**SpaceX's $75B IPO begins trading on Nasdaq (SPCX) at a ~$1.77T valuation - the largest IPO in history - underscoring how deeply AI-infrastructure-adjacent megacaps now dominate markets.**

## Model & Product Releases

- **OpenAI x Oracle**: As of June 11, enterprise customers can now access OpenAI's frontier models and Codex through their existing Oracle Universal Credits (UCM). Companies with Oracle cloud commitments can route AI workloads to OpenAI without a new vendor contract - a notable distribution win for OpenAI and a sign Oracle is positioning itself as a neutral AI compute layer. ([buildfastwithai.com](https://www.buildfastwithai.com/blogs/ai-news-today-june-11-2026))
- **Google Gemini 3.5**: Gemini 3.5 Flash went GA on May 19 (Google I/O) and is now the default in the Gemini app and AI Mode in Search, priced at $1.50/$9.00 per million tokens. Gemini 3.5 Pro is the bigger story to watch this month - Sundar Pichai signaled at I/O it's coming "next month." ([wavespeed.ai](https://wavespeed.ai/blog/posts/june-2026-ai-launch-wave/))
- **Anthropic Sonnet 4.8 (rumor)**: Speculation continues around a Claude Sonnet 4.8 / Opus 4.7 release ("Mythos"), based on strings found in a leaked source map shipped with a Claude Code npm package back in March. Reporting suggests a release before month-end, but nothing official yet. Treat as rumor. ([wavespeed.ai](https://wavespeed.ai/blog/posts/june-2026-ai-launch-wave/))

## Research & Technical Breakthroughs

- **CVPR 2026 awards** (announced June 9): Top honors include D4RT, a Google DeepMind / UCL / Oxford collaboration that reconstructs geometry and motion of dynamic 4D scenes from video - a step toward better world-modeling for robotics and video generation. Selected from 4,089 papers out of 16,092 submissions. ([newswise.com](https://www.newswise.com/articles/cvpr-2026-honors-the-year-s-most-innovative-computer-vision-and-ai-research))
- New agent benchmarks gaining traction: "Agents' Last Exam" (ALE) tests AI agents on long-horizon, economically valuable tasks across 13 industries; PaperBench evaluates whether agents can replicate state-of-the-art AI research papers. Both reflect the field's shift from raw model benchmarks toward real-world agent capability evaluation.

## Business, Funding & Deals

- **Anthropic**: Closed a $65B Series H in late May at a $965B post-money valuation, overtaking OpenAI's private valuation to become the world's most valuable AI startup. Confidentially filed its S-1 on June 1.
- **OpenAI**: Confidentially filed its S-1 on June 8 at an $852B valuation.
- **DeepSeek**: Reportedly nearing a $7.4B raise - one of the largest funding rounds ever for a Chinese startup, signaling continued capital flows into Chinese open-source AI despite export controls. ([pymnts.com](https://www.pymnts.com/artificial-intelligence-2/2026/deepseek-nears-deal-to-raise-7-4-billion-for-open-source-ai/))
- **CoreWeave**: Sold its first euro-denominated junk bonds (EUR2B / ~$2.3B) plus $1.25B in dollar bonds, expanding AI infrastructure debt financing into European credit markets. ([bloomberg.com](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-11/coreweave-ai-funding-goes-global-with-first-euro-junk-bond-deal))
- **Neura Robotics**: Secured up to $1.4B (June 11) to build out a "physical AI" platform - part of a broader surge in humanoid/embodied-AI funding.
- Context: four mega-rounds this cycle (OpenAI $122B, Anthropic $30B, xAI $20B, Waymo $16B) absorbed roughly 63% of all global VC.

## Policy & Regulation

- **Colorado AI Act** (SB 24-205) is set to take effect June 30, 2026. The DOJ joined xAI's lawsuit (filed April 9, joined April 24) seeking to block it, arguing the algorithmic-discrimination provisions could force models to produce inaccurate outputs. This is a key test case for the federal-vs-state AI regulation fight. [ONGOING]
- The White House's December 2025 executive order established an AI Litigation Task Force to challenge "onerous" state AI laws - the Colorado suit is its highest-profile action so far. [ONGOING]
