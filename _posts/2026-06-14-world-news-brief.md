**Trump says a US-Iran deal to end the "12 Day War" could be signed within days, even as Israel continues strikes on Hezbollah targets in south Beirut.**

## World News

- **US-Iran peace deal nearing signature.** President Trump said the two sides are close to formalizing the first stage of an agreement to end the recent Iran war, though Washington and Tehran reportedly differ on timing. Pakistan's prime minister, who has been mediating, said over the weekend a deal could come "in the next 24 hours." Why it matters: a signed deal would be the first major de-escalation since the "12 Day War" and could ease oil-market pressure (crude fell more than 3% on the earlier breakthrough headlines). Watch for whether Tehran's domestic hardliners accept the terms. (CBS News, Al Jazeera)

- **Israel strikes south Beirut.** Even as ceasefire diplomacy with Iran advances, Israeli forces hit targets in southern Beirut, underscoring that the broader regional conflict involving Hezbollah remains unresolved. Why it matters: a US-Iran deal alone won't necessarily calm the Israel-Lebanon front. (Al Jazeera)

- **Russia-Ukraine: deep strikes continue on both sides.** Ukrainian forces struck the "Crimean Titan" chemical plant (key military-supply input) and hit Russian oil infrastructure in Krasnodar and Volgograd regions, while Russia launched roughly 118 drones overnight at Ukraine, with new strikes on Dnipropetrovsk causing injuries. Why it matters: Kyiv's campaign against Russian energy infrastructure is increasingly effective and economically painful for Moscow, even as front-line attrition continues on both sides. (RBC-Ukraine, Ukrinform)

- **Anthropic forced to cut off Fable 5 and Mythos 5 models globally.** Anthropic disabled its top AI models for all users worldwide after a US government national-security order demanded it suspend foreign access; the company says it wasn't given specifics beyond a verbal claim that a safety-bypass method had been discovered. Why it matters: European officials are calling it a "wake-up call" on tech sovereignty and pushing for more investment in homegrown AI, while Anthropic has pushed back publicly against the order. (Euronews, TASS, Yahoo News)

## US News

- **Trump strike kills Tren de Aragua leader in Venezuela.** A US Southern Command strike, conducted with Venezuelan cooperation, killed Hector "Niño Guerrero" Guerrero Flores, the alleged top leader of the Tren de Aragua gang. Trump called it retribution for deaths he attributes to the gang's US members. Why it matters: signals continued aggressive use of military force against Latin American criminal networks and could affect US-Venezuela relations. (CNN, NPR, Washington Post)

- **Trump's name removed from Kennedy Center facade.** Workers began stripping Trump references from the building Saturday after a court-ordered Friday deadline. Why it matters: a visible marker of an ongoing legal/political dispute over control and branding of the institution.

- **Big domestic weekend moments.** The New York Knicks won the NBA championship, ending a 53-year title drought after a dramatic comeback. Separately, Trump attended a UFC event ("UFC Freedom 250") held on the White House South Lawn for his 80th birthday, and the US opened its World Cup campaign with a 4-1 win over Paraguay, paced by a two-goal game from Folarin Balogun - the first multi-goal World Cup game by an American since 1930.

## India News

- **LPG prices rise again.** A 14.2-kg domestic LPG cylinder rose by Rs 29 in Delhi to Rs 942 - the second increase in three months. Why it matters: adds to household cost pressures amid ongoing inflation concerns.

- **Active monsoon.** Kerala is seeing widespread, locally heavy rainfall, and Chennai faces warm, humid, cloudy conditions with rising rain chances - both consistent with an active southwest monsoon over southern India.

- **Diplomacy and labour policy.** India's delegation, led by Minister of State Shobha Karandlaje, took part in the 114th International Labour Conference in Geneva (through June 12), showcasing India's Labour Codes, social-protection expansion and Digital Public Infrastructure. Separately, India and Estonia held their 13th Foreign Office Consultations, with Estonia signaling interest in deeper trade, tech and defense ties.

- **Cricket.** Afghanistan's national cricket team is set to tour India this month for one Test and three ODIs.
