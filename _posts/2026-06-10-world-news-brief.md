**US launches fresh strikes on Iran after Apache helicopter downed near the Strait of Hormuz, pushing the fragile ceasefire to the brink of collapse.**

## World News

- **US-Iran-Israel conflict escalates again.** CENTCOM said it completed a new round of strikes on multiple targets in southern Iran's Hormozgan province (Sirik, Qeshm Island, Minab) after Iran downed a US Apache helicopter near the Strait of Hormuz. Tasnim reported at least six explosions and said roughly 20,000 Iranians lost drinking water access after strikes hit two reservoirs. President Trump said Iran "will pay the price" for delaying ceasefire talks and threatened to hit Iran "very hard" again. Iran vowed a "crushing and decisive response." Why it matters: this is the most serious flare-up since the April ceasefire was extended indefinitely, and risks a full collapse of the truce. Watch for: Iranian retaliation, Pakistan-mediated talks, and whether the Strait of Hormuz stays open to shipping. (Reuters via AA, NBC News, Al Jazeera, ABC News, CBS News)

- **Strait of Hormuz dispute.** Iran's military claimed the Strait, through which roughly 20% of global oil flows, is now closed to all marine traffic. US Central Command disputed this, saying commercial vessels continue to transit. Why it matters: even the threat of closure has driven extreme oil-price volatility (Brent and WTI remain roughly 36-50% above pre-war levels per Morgan Stanley/CNBC analysis, even as prices swung sharply on ceasefire headlines). OECD oil inventories are at 20-year lows, and the IEA has called this the largest oil supply disruption on record. (NBC News, CNBC)

- **Yemen: Houthi-government clashes intensify.** Heavy fighting broke out between Yemeni government forces and Houthi fighters in the western province of Hodeidah, per Yemeni state TV. Why it matters: a sign the regional conflict is widening beyond the Iran-Israel-US axis, with risk to Red Sea shipping routes. (Havana Times/AA roundup)

- **FIFA World Cup 2026 underway in North America.** Mexico hosts South Africa at Estadio Azteca; Canada faces Bosnia and Herzegovina in Toronto on June 12, with the US opening against Paraguay in the early hours of June 13. Separately, Somali referee Omar Abdulkadir Artan, set to be the first Somali official at a World Cup, was denied US entry and returned home from Istanbul. (ESPN, AA)

## US News

- **Iran strikes dominate the news cycle.** The Pentagon says strikes on Iran "in response to Iran's unwarranted and continued aggression" have been completed for now, with Trump signaling more action is possible. Defense Secretary Hegseth said the US will "negotiate with bombs" if needed. Why it matters: deepens US military commitment in the Middle East with no clear off-ramp visible yet. (NBC News, CBS News, Al Jazeera)

- **Inflation pressure building at home.** With oil inventories at multi-decade lows and prices elevated, US consumer prices are reportedly rising at their fastest pace in three years as the conflict drags on. Why it matters: renewed inflation could complicate Fed policy and add political pressure domestically. (NBC News)

## India News

- **Monsoon strengthens in the south, heatwave persists in the north.** Heavy rainfall continues across Kerala, Karnataka and Tamil Nadu, with Delhi-NCR expected to see rain later this week, while Rajasthan remains under severe heatwave conditions. Why it matters: an uneven monsoon onset affects agriculture planning and power demand simultaneously. (Vizzve roundup, Outlook India)

- **Cricket selection news.** Shreyas Iyer was named India's T20 captain and Vaibhav Sooryavanshi received a T20 call-up, while all-rounder Hardik Pandya was ruled out of the upcoming ODI series against Afghanistan with a fresh quadriceps strain. (Vizzve roundup)
