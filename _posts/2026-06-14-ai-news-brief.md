**Headline:** AI policy fights intensify as states push back on Trump's federal preemption push, while the funding arms race continues with DeepSeek reportedly closing in on a ~$7.4B round.

## Model & Product Releases

- No major frontier model drops confirmed in the last 24 hours. The bigger story remains Google's recent moves: Gemini 3.5 Flash went GA on May 19 ($1.50/$9.00 per million tokens), with Gemini 3.5 Pro teased for a June release without a firm date. Google also shipped Gemma 4, a new open-model family under Apache 2.0 aimed at reasoning and agentic workflows -- notable because it keeps pressure on Meta and Mistral in the open-weights tier. ([wavespeed.ai](https://wavespeed.ai/blog/posts/june-2026-ai-launch-wave/), [llm-stats.com](https://llm-stats.com/llm-updates))
- Microsoft and Google are both pushing new AI coding models aimed squarely at Anthropic's and OpenAI's code-assistant turf -- a sign the coding-agent market is becoming a four-way fight rather than a Claude/Copilot duopoly. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/01/microsoft-and-google-take-on-anthropic-and-openai-in-ai-coding-models.html))
- DeepSeek made its 75% V4-Pro price cut permanent, continuing the price war on inference costs. ([therundown.ai](https://www.therundown.ai/))

## Research & Technical Breakthroughs

- CVPR 2026 announced its award winners (Jun 9), drawn from over 16,000 submissions. Standout: D4RT, a Google DeepMind/UCL/Oxford collaboration that reconstructs geometry and motion of dynamic 4D scenes from video -- useful for robotics and world-model training. ([Newswise](https://www.newswise.com/articles/cvpr-2026-honors-the-year-s-most-innovative-computer-vision-and-ai-research))
- Google's TurboQuant (presented at ICLR 2026) combines vector rotation ("PolarQuant") with Johnson-Lindenstrauss compression to shrink KV-cache memory overhead -- a practical efficiency win for running very-long-context models cheaper. ([llm-stats.com](https://llm-stats.com/ai-news))
- NVIDIA validated NVFP4 pretraining on a 12B Mamba-Transformer model, another data point in the industry's push toward lower-precision training formats to cut compute costs. ([therundown.ai](https://www.therundown.ai/))

## Policy & Regulation

- The core ongoing fight [ONGOING]: following Trump's December 2025 executive order pushing for a uniform federal AI policy that preempts state law, states are "increasingly" moving ahead with their own AI regulations anyway -- a direct test of how much teeth that EO has. ([US News](https://www.usnews.com/news/us/articles/2026-06-14/trump-tried-to-block-state-ai-regulations-but-some-states-are-forging-ahead))
- xAI's lawsuit against California's AG over AB 2013 (the law requiring disclosure of training-data sources/composition) remains in litigation; commentators are flagging a broader constitutional "takings clause" angle on forced trade-secret disclosure for AI developers. ([Lawfare](https://www.lawfaremedia.org/article/ai-regulation-and-the-looming-problem-of-the-takings-clause))

([Mississippi Today](https://mississippitoday.org/2026/06/11/mississippi-judge-ousts-lawyers-ai/))
