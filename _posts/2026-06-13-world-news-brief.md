## World News

- **US-Iran deal nearing signature**: Pakistan's PM Shehbaz Sharif, mediating the talks, said a "final text" has been agreed and could be signed electronically within 24 hours. The memorandum reportedly includes a 60-day ceasefire extension, reopening of the Strait of Hormuz, dismantling of Iran's nuclear program, and an inspections regime in exchange for Iran ending support for regional militant groups. A senior Trump administration official put confidence at "80-85%," citing internal fractures in Tehran; Trump separately called Iranian negotiators "very dishonorable people" over leaked terms, suggesting last-mile friction remains. Why it matters: a deal would end a war that disrupted Middle East flights, shipping, and oil routes, and markets have already rallied on the prospect. Watch for: the actual signing and whether Iran's leadership ratifies it. (Al Jazeera, NBC News, CBS News, CNBC, Fox News)

- **Gaza ceasefire strained**: Despite the US-brokered ceasefire in place since last October, Israeli strikes killed at least 16 Palestinians in the past day - seven in a tent encampment in Gaza City and nine in a strike on a police station. Cumulative deaths from Israeli attacks since the ceasefire began are now around 950. Why it matters: highlights how fragile the truce remains even as US diplomatic attention is focused on Iran. Watch for: international pressure on Israel and whether casualty figures prompt a ceasefire review. (Democracy Now!)

- **SpaceX completes record IPO**: SpaceX debuted on Nasdaq (SPCX) and closed its first trading day up about 19-25%, valuing the company near $1.77 trillion off a $135/share offering - the largest IPO in history. Why it matters: validates the AI-infrastructure valuation thesis that Anthropic and OpenAI are reportedly relying on ahead of their own potential listings. (TheStreet, CNBC)

## US News

- **Kennedy Center Trump signage removal begins**: Workers started removing Trump's name from the Kennedy Center facade early Saturday, hours after a court-ordered Friday deadline. A federal judge had ruled the renaming unlawful. Why it matters: a rare judicial check on an administration branding move, and a visible symbol fight in Washington.

- **Asylum processing resumes after judicial rebuke**: The Trump administration said it will resume processing asylum and immigration applications for people from dozens of countries, after a federal judge found the prior indefinite hold - imposed after an Afghan national shot two National Guard members in DC - unlawful and arbitrary. Why it matters: reverses a blanket policy affecting thousands of pending cases. (Washington Post)

- **FISA Section 702 set to lapse**: House Democrats blocked a short-term reauthorization of Section 702, the warrantless foreign-surveillance authority used for counterterrorism. The program is set to expire for the first time since 2008. Why it matters: a major gap in intelligence-collection authority unless Congress acts quickly. (NBC News)

- **Intelligence leadership fight**: Trump said he'll nominate Jay Clayton as permanent Director of National Intelligence, after Democrats objected to acting chief Bill Pulte's lack of national security experience. (NBC News)

- **Markets and deals**: The S&P 500 rose 0.5%, Nasdaq +0.31%, Dow +0.7% on Friday on Iran-deal optimism. May PPI rose 1.1% - the largest monthly jump since November 2022. The DOJ is reported to have cleared the Paramount-WBD merger. An AI-adoption index (Ramp) reportedly shows Anthropic overtaking OpenAI in US business adoption for the first time. (CNBC, TheStreet, Schwab)

- **World Cup kickoff**: The US opened its home World Cup campaign with a 4-1 win over Paraguay at SoFi Stadium (Balogun brace, Reyna goal, Pulisic assists) - the first time the US has hosted the tournament in 32 years, co-hosting with Canada and Mexico. Canada also picked up a point with a late equalizer. (ESPN, CBS News)

Why it matters: sets the tone for the government's messaging ahead of future state elections. (India TV News, Business Standard)

Why it matters: early monsoon disruptions to aviation and city infrastructure in major metros. (NewsX, India.com)
