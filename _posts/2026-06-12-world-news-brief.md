**US and Iran traded a second day of strikes, Iran declared the Strait of Hormuz closed to all shipping, and oil prices jumped over $2 a barrel as the April ceasefire effectively collapsed.**

## World News

- **US-Iran conflict escalates sharply.** The US carried out a second consecutive day of airstrikes on Iranian targets on June 11, with Trump saying "we hit them hard yesterday, and we're going to hit them again hard today." Iran's foreign ministry said the strikes had "effectively rendered the ceasefire of April 8, 2026 meaningless" and announced the Strait of Hormuz - a chokepoint for roughly 20% of global seaborne oil - is closed to all traffic. Jordan intercepted five missiles near a major airbase, and Kuwait and Bahrain reported their air defenses engaging incoming fire. Why it matters: a sustained closure of Hormuz could push oil toward $100/barrel and add meaningfully to global inflation. Watch for further strikes and whether Gulf states get pulled deeper into the conflict. (Reuters, NPR, Al Jazeera)

- **Oil markets spike on Hormuz closure.** Brent crude rose about 2.5% to roughly $95/barrel and WTI climbed to about $92.6/barrel in the hours after Iran's closure announcement, with analysts warning of $100+ oil if disruptions persist. Why it matters: higher energy prices feed directly into inflation and could complicate central bank rate paths worldwide. (CNBC, Business Standard, OilPrice.com)

- **US naval blockade hits commercial shipping - Indian sailors killed.** US Central Command says it disabled the Palau-flagged tanker M/T Settebello in the Gulf of Oman for violating the blockade on Iranian ports, killing three Indian crew members (Aditya Sharma, Shivanand Chaurasiya, Patnala Suresh). Iran called the strike "armed robbery and state piracy." Why it matters: the blockade is now causing third-country civilian casualties, raising the diplomatic stakes well beyond the US-Iran dyad. (CBS News, Tribune India)

- **Xi Jinping visits North Korea.** China's president made his first trip to Pyongyang since 2019 on June 8-9, meeting Kim Jong Un and pledging to elevate ties "to a higher level." Why it matters: the visit signals Beijing reasserting itself as North Korea's primary partner amid deepening Russia-North Korea cooperation and rising tension with Japan. (NPR, CNN, Al Jazeera)

- **Taiwan awaits US arms decision.** Taipei is still waiting on approval of a $14 billion US arms package, with continued uncertainty over Washington's long-term defense commitment to the island. Why it matters: any delay or scaling-back would be read in the region as a signal about US bandwidth given the Iran conflict. (Wikipedia current events)

## US News

- **Trump links Iran war to inflation outlook.** The president argued that recent inflation figures point to a bigger decline once the Iran conflict ends, even as oil prices climb on the Hormuz closure. Why it matters: ties the economic narrative directly to how/when the Iran conflict resolves. (NBC News)

- **Supreme Court in year-end crunch.** With 23 of 58 argued cases still undecided as the term winds down, major rulings are pending on: birthright citizenship (challenge to Trump's executive order), Trump's authority to fire Fed governor Lisa Cook and FTC commissioners, mail-in ballot deadlines (Watson v. RNC), party coordinated-spending limits, TPS terminations for Syrians and Haitians, and transgender athletes' Title IX claims. Why it matters: several of these could reshape executive power and election rules ahead of the midterms. Decisions expected through end of June/early July. (NPR, SCOTUSblog, Axios)

- **World Cup kicks off.** The 2026 FIFA World Cup opened June 11 at a renovated Estadio Azteca in Mexico City, with hosts Mexico beating South Africa 2-0 (first goal by Julian Quinones). South Korea also defeated Czechia on day one. Why it matters: a record 48-team, 1,248-player tournament spanning the US, Mexico and Canada kicks off amid heightened security given global tensions. (CNN, NBC News, ESPN)

- **California primary results set general-election matchups.** Republican Steve Hilton will face Democrat Xavier Becerra in November following the June 2 primaries; competitive GOP incumbent races are also shaping up in Maine and Nevada, with a crowded MAGA field vying for the South Carolina governorship. Why it matters: early signals for the 2026 midterm landscape. (NBC News)

## India News

- **Three Indian sailors killed in US strike on tanker.** India lodged a "strong protest" after three Indian crew members died when US forces struck the Palau-flagged tanker M/T Settebello for allegedly violating the Iran ports blockade. MEA spokesperson Randhir Jaiswal said "these attacks must cease" and called for dialogue. Iran separately condemned the strike on what it called an Indian commercial vessel. Why it matters: drags India into the fallout of the US-Iran conflict and raises questions about the safety of Indian maritime workers in the Gulf. (CBS News, Tribune India, Common Dreams)

- **Quad maritime surveillance push.** India's Ambassador to the US discussed Indo-Pacific cooperation including a new Quad maritime-domain-awareness initiative, expressing optimism about India-US supply-chain partnerships. Why it matters: comes against the backdrop of Gulf shipping risk and broader Indo-Pacific positioning vis-a-vis China. (News9live)

- **All 28 chief ministers join NITI Aayog meeting with PM Modi.** A full governing-council meeting brought together the Centre and all state CMs. Why it matters: signals coordination on policy priorities heading into the next fiscal cycle; no major announcements reported yet. (News9live)

- **West Bengal Rajya Sabha nominee controversy.** A letter was sent to West Bengal's Chief Minister raising concerns about an RS nominee's alleged links to extremist organizations operating in Bangladesh. Why it matters: adds to pre-election friction in West Bengal. (News9live)
